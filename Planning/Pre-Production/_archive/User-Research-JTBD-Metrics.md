# User Research, JTBD, and Metrics (Global)

## 1) Primary User Definition

Primary: people who want to create content with a face/avatar, but not their own face/voice.

## 2) Segment Hypotheses (Ranked)

1. Faceless educators and explainers
2. Solo creators building niche channels
3. Small agencies managing multiple identities
4. Brands experimenting with synthetic presenters

## 3) Jobs-To-Be-Done (JTBD)

### Functional
- Create a believable digital avatar quickly.
- Produce consistent content at scale.
- Convert trends/events into publishable narratives.
- Improve outputs through targeted edits.

### Emotional
- Feel confident publishing without personal exposure.
- Feel ownership and consistency in avatar identity.
- Avoid embarrassment from low-quality AI outputs.

### Social
- Build audience trust and familiarity with recurring avatar identity.
- Publish consistently without appearing low-effort or generic.

## 4) Core User Risks

- Fear of generic/robotic output
- Fear of inconsistency across videos
- Fear of platform violations
- Fear of long render times and failed jobs

## 5) Success Metrics (Global)

### North-Star Candidate
- Weekly Published Videos per Active User (WPVAU)

### Onboarding Metrics
- Time-to-first-avatar (from sign-up to avatar card created)
- Time-to-first-artifact (from sign-up to first script or video draft)
- Time-to-first-publish (from sign-up to first published/exported video)
- Onboarding completion rate (% who complete guided flow)
- Onboarding drop-off step (where users abandon)

### Product Metrics
- Draft-to-render conversion
- Render success rate
- Regeneration frequency per video
- Publish success rate
- Content strategy generation rate (how many users generate strategies)
- Strategy adherence rate (% of published videos that follow generated strategy)
- Avatars deployed vs configured (adoption of Deploy lifecycle)
- Theme customization rate (% of users who change accent color)

### UX Metrics
- Task completion rate for core flows
- Error recovery rate (% of errors that lead to successful retry)
- Time-on-task for key operations (avatar creation, script editing, video review)
- Search usage frequency and success rate

### Quality Metrics
- Avatar consistency score
- Visual continuity score (segment boundaries)
- Voice continuity score
- Human quality pass rate

### Trust/Safety Metrics
- Policy-block false positive/negative rates
- Moderation escalation rate
- Incident rate per 1,000 outputs

### Retention / Churn Indicators
- Week-1, week-4, week-12 retention by segment
- Days between first and second published video
- Avatar reuse rate (returning to same avatar vs creating new ones)
- Automation adoption rate (% of users who set up at least one automation)
- Churn risk signals: declining publish frequency, render failure without retry, zero logins for 7+ days
